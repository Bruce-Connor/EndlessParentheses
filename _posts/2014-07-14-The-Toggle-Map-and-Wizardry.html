I love intuitive keymaps. Some are so perfect, you just can't avoid mouthing the words every time you hit that blissful combo. A wizard murmuring an incantation under his breath as his fingers draw the arcane patterns.

Here's a keymap that toggles some options which tend to be useful throughout a session.

{% highlight cl %} (define-prefix-command 'toggle-map) (define-key ctl-x-map "t" #'toggle-map) (define-key toggle-map "c" 'column-number-mode) (define-key toggle-map "d" 'toggle-debug-on-error) (define-key toggle-map "e" 'toggle-debug-on-error) (define-key toggle-map "f" 'auto-fill-mode) (define-key toggle-map "l" 'toggle-truncate-lines) (define-key toggle-map "q" 'toggle-debug-on-quit) (define-key toggle-map "r" 'read-only-mode) (define-key toggle-map "t" 'tt/toggle-theme) {% endhighlight %}

There are eight keys being defined there, most of which aren't even used every day, but I know I'll never forget a single one. That is the beauty of intuitiveness.

Doesn't "Emacs toggle column" just roll off your tongue as you're typing C-x t c? I feel like I'm commanding the strands of reality, but that could just be my D&D past taking the better of me.
